A.R. Rahman

The Kohinoor of Indian Music

Rahman at Coke Studio

A living legend A.R.Rahman is an Indian composer, singer-songwriter, music producer, musician and a philanthropist.

Born a hindu as A.S. Dileep Kumar Allah Rakha Rahman converted to Islam in 1989.

He is skilled in Carnatic music, Western and Hindustani classical music and is know for amalgating and experimenting with traditional and new electronic sounds and technology.

Director Baz Luhrmann said in his praise,

I had come to the music of A. R. Rahman through the emotional and haunting score of Bombay and the wit and celebration of Lagaan. But the more of AR's music I encountered the more I was to be amazed at the sheer diversity of styles: from swinging brass bands to triumphant anthems; from joyous pop to West-End musicals. Whatever the style, A. R. Rahman's music always possesses a profound sense of humanity and spirit, qualities that inspire me the most.

Some of his most appreciated albums are :
  • Roja
  • Bombay
  • Dil Se
  • Lagaan
  • Swades
  • Rang De Basanti
  • Rockstar
Rahman has got high critical appreciations which have earned him several prestigious awards. Some of them are :
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Original Score
  • BAFTA Award for Best Film Music
  • Academy Awards for Best Original Score and Best Original Song (shared with Gulzar)
  • Grammys for Best Compilation Soundtrack Album and Best Song Written for Visual Media
  • Padma Bhushan
